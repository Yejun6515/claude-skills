PT Krakatau POSCO — Equipment Status & Phase 2 Expansion Plan

Cilegon integrated steel works, Indonesia — current installed equipment and Phase 2 expansion outlook
Date : 2026-06-09 Basis : PTKR (Primetals Korea) email (BonKyoung Koo) + public sources To : Markus / Tanaka-san

Executive summary

  • PT Krakatau POSCO (PTKP) is a joint venture between POSCO and Indonesia's Krakatau Steel (PTKS) — Indonesia's first blast-furnace (BF) based integrated steel works. In operation since December 2013, with 3.0 mtpy of crude steel.
  • Current lines: ironmaking (sinter · coke · BF #1) → steelmaking (BOF) → continuous casting → plate mill and hot rolling plant (HSM). Products are slab, plate, and hot-rolled coil (HRC).
  • Phase 2 expansion momentum is building: the Indonesian President's visit to Korea (Mar 2026) and POSCO Holdings Chairman Chang's visit to Indonesia (May 2026) conveyed POSCO's requirements and built consensus to proceed.
  • Concept: upstream largely the same spec as Phase 1 (cost reduction via value engineering and some Chinese-sourced equipment) + HRM 1.5 → 3.0 mtpy uprate + one PLTCM (~1.4–1.8 mtpy) + one CGL/CVGL (0.55 mtpy).
  • Feasibility study (FS) targeted for completion October 2026 (Indian/Chinese engineering firm or POSCO E&C). Completion target 2030 or earlier — linkage with the Phase 1 BF relining (2030) is the key variable.

Note on the hot rolling plant (HRM)
The "HRM (1.5 mtpy)" in question was originally the second hot strip mill (HSM #2, started May 2021; max width 1,650 mm, min thickness 1.40 mm) that SMS group supplied to PTKS. It was transferred into Krakatau POSCO via an in-kind contribution agreement dated 28 November 2022. The line is designed to expand to 4.0 mtpy, and it is precisely this facility that is the target of the Phase 2 "1.5 → 3.0 mtpy uprate".

3Phase 2 expansion plan (per the email)

Background — Through the Indonesian President's visit to Korea (March 2026) and POSCO Holdings Chairman Chang's visit to Indonesia (May 2026), POSCO's requirements were sufficiently conveyed to the Indonesian side. As a result, consensus is forming on the need to proceed with the Phase 2 expansion.

3.1 Upstream

  • Effectively the same spec as Phase 1 (replicating the integrated line, i.e. a second blast furnace).
  • Focus on cost reduction: ① apply value engineering, ② source some equipment from China.
  • On completion, crude steel expands 3.0 → 6.0 mtpy (toward a second blast furnace).

3.2 Downstream (HRM & cold rolling / coating)

EquipmentCapacity (concept)Description
HRM (hot strip) uprate expand 1.5 → 3.0 mtpy Concept of doubling existing hot-strip capacity (the current 1.5 mtpy line is designed for up to 4.0 mtpy).
One PLTCM new ≈1.4 – 1.8 mtpy Pickling Line Tandem Cold Mill (combined pickling + cold rolling). The core line that rolls HRC into cold-rolled (CR) sheet.
One CVGL / CGL new 0.55 mtpy Continuous Galvanizing Line. Galvanizes cold-rolled sheet (GI/GA) to produce surface-treated steel for automotive, appliance and construction use.

※ Adding a PLTCM and CGL signals PTKP's move from a slab / plate / hot-strip focus toward cold rolling and coating (high-value flat products).

4Key milestones / timeline

  • 2026.03 — Indonesian President's visit to Korea (occasion to convey POSCO's requirements)
  • 2026.05 — POSCO Holdings Chairman Chang visits Indonesia → consensus to pursue Phase 2
  • 2026.10 — FS completion target
  • 2030 — Phase 1 blast-furnace relining scheduled
  • 2030 or earlier — Phase 2 completion target (considering an earlier date to integrate smoothly with the Phase 1 relining)
Key scheduling variable — Because the Phase 1 blast furnace goes into relining in 2030, the intent is to align Phase 2 completion to 2030 or earlier to minimise the production gap and integrate smoothly with the relining schedule. In effect, 2030 may act as the de-facto deadline.

5Primetals perspective / implications

Opportunity

  • New downstream lines (PLTCM · CGL) are a Primetals strength — potential to win the cold-rolling / coating line package.
  • The HRM 1.5 → 3.0 mtpy uprate drives demand for rolling and auxiliary-equipment revamps / additions.
  • Upstream second-BF and steelmaking additions create demand for ancillary plant (gas cleaning, automation, environmental).
  • 2030 deadline → early engagement from the FS stage (~Oct 2026) is advantageous for locking in specifications.

Cautions / risks

  • Upstream follows a "same spec as Phase 1 + cost reduction" stance → some Chinese sourcing is explicit. Expect intensified price competition.
  • FS execution by an Indian/Chinese engineering firm or POSCO E&C → influences the contracting structure and specifications.
  • Existing hot strip is SMS group and plate is MHMM → consider interfaces with the legacy equipment.
  • Still at the conceptual (study) stage — capacity, configuration and schedule are all subject to change.
